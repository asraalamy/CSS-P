Mazar-e-Sharif

Things to know about Mazar-e-Sharif

Mazar-e-Sharif, the capital of Balkh Province, is located in northern Afghanistan. It is the fourth largest city in Afghanistan after Kabul, Herat, and Kandahar. Mazar-e-Sharif is famous for the tomb attributed to Ali ibn Abi Talib, or the Prophet Zoroaster.

Herat

Things to know about Herat

Herat Province is one of the largest provinces in Afghanistan, with the city of Herat as its capital. Herat is the second largest and most populous province in Afghanistan. It borders Turkmenistan to the north, Ghor and Badghis to the east, Farah to the south, and Iran to the west. Herat is one of the historical, green, and pleasant provinces of Afghanistan, with fertile valleys irrigated by the Harirud River.
